Yedora is a Non-Profit-Organisation supporting welfare of juveniles in detention, through service orientation, preventive advocacy & patriotism towards crime-free societies.

YEDORA is a non-profit organization dedicated to supporting the welfare of juveniles in detention. Here in Ghana, we've organized initiatives like menstrual hygiene education programs for adolescent girls at the Shelter for Abused Children and Juvenile Girls Correctional Centre in Accra.
Our efforts aim to promote juvenile justice and create crime-free societies through :


  • Service Orientation: Providing essential services to juveniles in detention, such as education, vocational training, and mentorship.
  • Preventive Advocacy: Advocating for policies and practices that prevent juvenile delinquency and promote rehabilitation.
  • Patriotism: Fostering a sense of national pride and responsibility among juveniles to encourage positive contributions to society.

Some areas of focus for Yedora are :

  • Juvenile Justice Advocacy: Collaborating with organizations to improve juvenile justice systems and promote rehabilitation over punishment.
  • Education and Vocational Training: Providing educational programs and vocational training to equip juveniles with skills for successful reintegration into society.
  • Mentorship and Counseling: Offering mentorship and counseling services to support juveniles in detention and help them develop positive relationships.

Our Vision

We envision a society where juveniles are treated with dignity and respect, and have the opportunity to reach their full potential. We believe that every juvenile deserves access to education, vocational training, and mentorship, and that with the right support, they can become productive members of society.

Our Mission

Our mission is to provide essential services to juveniles in detention, advocate for policy reforms that promote rehabilitation over punishment, and foster a sense of national pride among juveniles. We believe that by working together, we can create a safer, more just society for all.

Our Values

  • Compassion: We believe in treating juveniles with dignity and respect, and providing them with the support they need to thrive.
  • Empowerment: We believe in empowering juveniles with the skills and knowledge they need to become productive members of society.
  • Advocacy: We believe in advocating for policy reforms that promote rehabilitation over punishment, and that support the welfare of juveniles.
  • Patriotism: We believe in fostering a sense of national pride among juveniles, and encouraging them to make positive contributions to society.

Our Programmes

  • Education and Vocational Training: We provide educational programs and vocational training to equip juveniles with the skills they need to succeed in life.
  • Mentorship and Counseling: We offer mentorship and counseling services to support juveniles in detention and help them develop positive relationships.
  • Advocacy and Policy Reform: We advocate for policy reforms that promote rehabilitation over punishment, and that support the welfare of juveniles.



Our Impacts/ Success Stories:
#Story 01

On 1st Jan 2018, the Founder embarked on the first official Donation of Sanitary Products to about 150 inmates of the Senior Correctional Center, Dzorwulu-Accra.


Impact: The Founder through the interactions got first hand information about the real needs and constraints affecting the inmates of the senior correctional center. This experience shaped the Mission and Objectives of the Yedora NPO.



#Story 02

In Nov 2018, a physical abuse case within the Ga West District Assembly involving a teenage girl caught our attention. We offered diverse forms of support including hospital bill payment.


Also, We followed up from the time she got integrated into the home of her parental parent she recovered.


During the festive Christmas break, about 12 volunteers connected with her family and we had a joyous Get-together. The family appreciated our cash and kind gifts.




Impact:The passion which poked our first engagement with a victim of physical abuse was a stepping stone for future outreaches.


#Story 03

COVID-19 was a challenging phase for everyone. We didn't rest on our oars, We raised funds from volunteers and friends. We served 170 packs of hot jollof rice at lunchtime. Additionally, We donated cup cakes, 12 crates of eggs and 10 cartons of bottled water. Really, love can make a Difference!!




#Story 04

With Green Generation as a worthy partner, We acted on a Menstrual Hygiene Campaign within the South Labone Female Shelter in Nov. 2020. The interactive educational session was Informative. Finally the gifting of 110 sanitary pouches (each pouch containing a small towel, soap, wipes, waste bag, sanitary pad) was the icing on the cake.



#Story 05

In recognition that most juveniles living in rural areas have deficit in terms of literacy accuracy, We birthed The Literacy Wing under the mother Organization in 2022.


To mark the 10th birthday of Yael E. Dakwa Owusu (YEDO), he donated 100 assorted reading books to "the PCR" an NGO based in Jachie Pramso in Bosomtwe Constituency of Ashanti Region. We are positive the performance in Literacy of basic school pupils will improve.... Nov 04, 2022.




#Story 06

To demystify Science as a subject among basic school students, a Mentee, Dr Andrew Sarkodie-Appiah donated 36 Integrated Science Workbook through YEDORA NPO on 20th October 2023.


Impact: 36 peers in Basic 8 had the opportunity to use the well structured Integrated Science Workbooks as study guide.
